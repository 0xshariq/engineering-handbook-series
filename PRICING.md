## Part I — Pricing Philosophy & Commercial Framework

### 1. Purpose of This Manual

This manual defines the official pricing framework for all Engineering Handbook publications.

It establishes:

- pricing principles;
- pricing methodology;
- pricing classifications;
- commercial policies; and
- pricing governance.

This manual explains **how prices are determined**, not what legal rights purchasers receive.

For licensing, copyright, and usage rights, refer to the **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual**.

---

### 2. Pricing Philosophy

All pricing decisions within the Engineering Handbook ecosystem follow these principles:

- Educational value comes before commercial value.
- Prices should be fair, transparent, and consistent.
- Similar publications should receive similar pricing.
- Pricing decisions should be based on objective evaluation criteria.
- Commercial sustainability should support continued handbook development.
- Policies should remain scalable for future publications.

---

### 3. Educational Accessibility

Pricing should encourage legitimate learning while maintaining sustainable publication.

Where reasonable, pricing policies should improve accessibility through initiatives such as student pricing, institutional programs, regional pricing, promotional offers, or educational partnerships.

---

### 4. Commercial Sustainability

Revenue supports the continued development of the Engineering Handbook ecosystem, including:

- research;
- technical verification;
- editing;
- illustrations;
- future revisions;
- new publications; and
- long-term maintenance.

Commercial sustainability should never be used to justify arbitrary pricing.

---

### 5. Relationship with the Licensing Manual

This manual governs **commercial pricing**.

The **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual** governs:

- copyright;
- licensing;
- ownership;
- redistribution;
- institutional rights; and
- legal usage.

Pricing does not grant legal rights, and licensing policies do not determine commercial prices unless an approved pricing policy explicitly requires it.

For complete licensing policies, refer to the appropriate sections of the **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual**.

- price ranges;

Pricing policies may be updated through official revisions of this manual.

## Part II — Pricing Methodology

### 1. Overview

#### Purpose

The Engineering Handbook pricing methodology provides a structured process for determining the commercial price of every publication.

Rather than assigning prices arbitrarily, each handbook is evaluated using standardized assessment criteria. The combined evaluation determines its pricing class and recommended price range.

---

### 2. Pricing Workflow

Every publication follows the same pricing workflow:

1. Determine the publication type.
2. Assess the knowledge level.
3. Evaluate technical depth.
4. Evaluate engineering complexity.
5. Evaluate research and development effort.
6. Evaluate production effort.
7. Assign a pricing class.
8. Determine the recommended price range.
9. Apply applicable pricing policies (regional pricing, student pricing, bundle pricing, etc.).

This workflow ensures that pricing decisions remain consistent across all Engineering Handbook publications.

---

### 3. Evaluation Factors

Commercial pricing is determined using multiple evaluation factors rather than a single characteristic.

Primary evaluation factors include:

| Factor | Purpose |
|---------|----------|
| Knowledge Level | Educational difficulty |
| Technical Depth | Engineering complexity |
| Research Effort | Time and verification required |
| Production Effort | Writing, editing, diagrams, formatting |
| Publication Scope | Breadth of covered material |
| Long-Term Maintenance | Expected update effort |

No single factor independently determines the final price.

---

### 4. Factors Not Used for Pricing

The following characteristics SHALL NOT independently determine commercial pricing:

- page count;
- publication order;
- handbook popularity;
- expected sales;
- development duration;
- marketing considerations; and
- author preference.

These characteristics may provide context but are not pricing criteria.

---

### 5. Weighted Evaluation

Each evaluation factor contributes to the overall commercial assessment.

Different factors may carry different weights depending on the publication type.

The detailed weighting system is defined in later sections of this manual.

---

### 6. Pricing Classification

After evaluation, each publication is assigned to a pricing class.

The pricing class represents the commercial value of the publication rather than its exact selling price.

Detailed pricing classes are defined in **Part III — Knowledge-Level Classification** and **Part IV — Standard Pricing Framework**.

---

### 7. Final Price Determination

The final selling price is determined by combining:

- pricing class;
- standard price range;
- edition type;
- regional pricing adjustments;
- applicable discounts;
- bundle policies; and
- commercial promotions, where approved.

This ensures that every published price follows a documented and repeatable methodology.

---

### 8. Decision Summary

The pricing methodology ensures that every commercial price is:

- objective;
- transparent;
- consistent;
- scalable; and
- supported by documented evaluation criteria.

## Part III — Knowledge-Level Classification

### 1. Purpose

Knowledge-Level Classification groups Engineering Handbook publications according to the level of knowledge and experience expected from the reader.

It helps ensure that publications with similar educational difficulty receive comparable commercial pricing.

Knowledge level is one pricing factor and SHALL NOT independently determine the final price.

---

### 2. Classification Levels

| Level | Target Audience | Description |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Foundation | Beginners, students | Introduces core concepts with little or no prior knowledge required. |
| Intermediate | Learners with fundamentals | Builds practical skills and expands foundational knowledge. |
| Advanced | Experienced learners | Covers advanced engineering concepts, implementation details, and deeper technical understanding. |
| Professional | Industry professionals | Focuses on production systems, real-world engineering practices, optimization, and large-scale implementations. |
| Specialist | Subject-matter experts | Covers niche domains, research topics, or highly specialized engineering knowledge. |

---

### 3. Classification Criteria

Knowledge level is determined using factors such as:

- prerequisite knowledge;
- technical vocabulary;
- conceptual difficulty;
- implementation complexity;
- expected reader experience; and
- learning objectives.

No single criterion independently determines the classification.

---

### 4. Multi-Level Publications

Some publications may contain material spanning multiple knowledge levels.

In such cases, the classification should reflect the **highest sustained level** of the publication rather than isolated advanced chapters.

---

### 5. Relationship with Pricing

Higher knowledge levels generally require greater research, verification, and production effort.

However, knowledge level alone does not determine commercial pricing.

Final pricing also considers:

- technical depth;
- engineering complexity;
- research effort;
- production effort; and
- publication scope.

---

### 6. Classification Review

Knowledge-level classifications should be reviewed whenever a publication undergoes major revisions, remastering, or significant content expansion.

If the educational scope changes substantially, the pricing classification may also require reassessment.

---

### 7. Decision Summary

Knowledge-Level Classification provides a consistent educational framework for evaluating Engineering Handbook publications.

It improves pricing consistency while remaining only one component of the overall commercial pricing methodology.

---

## Part IV — Standard Pricing Framework

### 1. Purpose

This framework converts the evaluated technical value of a handbook into a recommended commercial price.

Pricing is determined in two stages:

1. Assign a pricing level.
2. Apply customer-category pricing.

---

### 2. Standard Personal Pricing

The following ranges represent the **base Personal pricing**.

| Level | Recommended Personal Range |
|------|---------------------------:|
| Level 1 — Foundation | ₹1,499–₹2,999 |
| Level 2 — Intermediate | ₹3,000–₹6,500 |
| Level 3 — Advanced | ₹6,500–₹12,000 |
| Level 4 — Expert | ₹12,000–₹22,000 |
| Level 5 — Research & Frontier | ₹22,000–₹40,000 |
| Level 6 — Specialized / Exceptional | ₹40,000+ (requires documented justification) |

Student pricing is derived from the approved Personal price through the official student discount policy.

---

### 3. Customer Categories

| Category | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Student | Verified students |
| Personal | Individual readers |
| Educational Institution | Schools, colleges, universities and libraries |
| Enterprise | Companies and organizations |
| Commercial Training | Training providers and coaching institutes |
| Content Creator | Commercial educators, instructors and publishers |

---

### 4. Commercial Pricing Multipliers

Commercial pricing is derived from the approved Personal price.

| Category | Recommended Multiplier |
|----------|-----------------------:|
| Personal | ×1.0 |
| Student | ×0.75–×0.90 |
| Educational Institution | ×3–×6 |
| Enterprise | ×8–×20 |
| Commercial Training | ×10–×30 |
| Content Creator | ×5–×15 |

Actual pricing depends on the applicable licensing model and approved commercial policy.

---

### 5. Price Adjustments

Approved adjustments may include:

- regional pricing;
- bundle pricing;
- promotional pricing;
- edition upgrades; and
- limited-time offers.

---

### 6. Exceptional Pricing

Level 6 pricing and exceptional commercial pricing require documented approval.

---

### 7. Decision Summary

Every handbook receives:

1. a technical pricing level;
2. an approved Personal price; and
3. a customer-category price where applicable.

## Part V — Commercial Products

### 1. Purpose

The Engineering Handbook ecosystem may include multiple commercial products beyond standard handbook volumes.

This section defines the categories of commercial products and the pricing policies applicable to each.

---

### 2. Standard Handbook Volumes

Standard handbook volumes are the primary educational publications within the Engineering Handbook Series.

Pricing follows the standard methodology defined in Parts II–IV of this manual.

---

### 3. Companion Publications

Companion publications supplement one or more handbook volumes.

Examples include:

- workbooks;
- lab manuals;
- exercise books;
- project collections;
- quick-reference guides; and
- revision guides.

Companion publications may be priced independently or offered as part of a bundle.

---

### 4. Digital Products

Digital commercial products may include:

- PDF editions;
- eBook editions;
- interactive editions;
- downloadable resources; and
- digital toolkits.

Pricing may differ from printed editions depending on the distribution model and commercial strategy.

---

### 5. Bundles

Multiple publications may be offered together as commercial bundles.

Bundle pricing should provide a reasonable commercial benefit compared with purchasing each publication separately while maintaining overall pricing consistency.

---

### 6. New Editions

New editions that introduce significant educational improvements may receive revised pricing following the standard pricing methodology.

Minor corrections or maintenance updates should not normally require a new pricing evaluation.

---

### 7. Special Editions

Special editions may include:

- collector's editions;
- anniversary editions;
- signed editions;
- premium print editions; and
- limited releases.

These editions may receive independent commercial pricing where justified.

---

### 8. Future Product Categories

The Engineering Handbook ecosystem may introduce additional commercial products in the future.

New product categories shall follow the pricing principles and governance framework established by this manual unless an officially approved policy provides otherwise.

---

### 9. Decision Summary

Every commercial product within the Engineering Handbook ecosystem shall be assigned an appropriate pricing policy based on its product category while remaining consistent with the overall pricing methodology.

## Part VI — Institutional & Enterprise Pricing

### 1. Purpose

This part defines pricing policies for organizations purchasing Engineering Handbook publications for educational, training, or commercial purposes.

These policies apply in addition to the standard pricing framework described in this manual.

---

### 2. Individual Pricing

Individual pricing applies to personal purchases made by a single reader for personal educational use.

Unless otherwise stated, all published prices represent individual pricing.

For permitted usage rights, refer to the **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual**.

---

### 3. Student Pricing

Student pricing may be offered to improve educational accessibility.

Eligibility, verification requirements, and applicable discounts are determined by official pricing policies and may change over time.

---

### 4. Institutional Pricing

Institutional pricing applies to organizations such as:

- schools;
- colleges;
- universities;
- libraries;
- educational institutions; and
- training centers.

Pricing may vary based on the number of authorized users, purchased publications, or applicable institutional agreements.

Licensing rights are governed separately by the **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual**.

---

### 5. Enterprise Pricing

Enterprise pricing applies to commercial organizations purchasing publications for professional or organizational use.

Pricing may consider factors such as:

- organization size;
- deployment scale;
- number of licensed users; and
- commercial agreements.

Commercial pricing does not modify licensing rights.

---

### 6. Bulk Purchases

Organizations purchasing multiple copies or multiple publications may qualify for approved bulk pricing.

Bulk pricing policies should remain transparent, consistent, and commercially sustainable.

---

### 7. Regional Pricing

Regional pricing may be introduced where necessary to improve educational accessibility while maintaining commercial fairness.

Regional adjustments should follow officially approved pricing policies and remain consistent across comparable markets.

---

### 8. Promotional Pricing

Temporary promotional pricing may be offered for approved commercial or educational initiatives.

Examples include:

- launch offers;
- seasonal promotions;
- educational campaigns;
- conference events; and
- limited-time discounts.

Promotional pricing should not replace the standard pricing framework.

---

### 9. Decision Summary

Institutional and enterprise pricing extends the standard pricing framework to support organizations, educational institutions, and commercial customers while maintaining fairness, consistency, and documented pricing governance.

## Part VII — Pricing Governance

### 1. Purpose

Pricing governance establishes the rules and responsibilities for creating, reviewing, approving, and maintaining pricing policies throughout the Engineering Handbook ecosystem.

Its purpose is to ensure that pricing decisions remain consistent, transparent, and well documented.

---

### 2. Pricing Authority

All official pricing decisions shall be approved through the Engineering Handbook pricing governance process.

Unofficial, undocumented, or ad hoc pricing decisions should not be considered part of the official pricing framework.

---

### 3. Pricing Reviews

Pricing should be reviewed whenever significant changes occur, including:

- major handbook revisions;
- new editions;
- new product categories;
- pricing framework updates; or
- significant commercial changes.

Routine reviews help maintain consistency across the handbook ecosystem.

---

### 4. Pricing Revisions

Approved pricing changes should be documented through official revisions of this manual or the Official Pricing Schedule.

Previous pricing decisions should remain traceable for historical reference.

---

### 5. Pricing Exceptions

Exceptions to the standard pricing framework may be approved when justified.

Examples include:

- limited-time commercial initiatives;
- educational partnerships;
- pilot programs;
- special releases; and
- exceptional publishing circumstances.

Every approved exception should be documented with its purpose and duration.

---

### 6. Official Pricing Schedule

This manual defines **how prices are determined**.

The actual monetary prices for publications are maintained separately in the **Official Pricing Schedule**, allowing prices to be updated without revising this manual.

---

### 7. Relationship with Other Manuals

This manual governs commercial pricing only.

Matters relating to copyright, licensing, ownership, redistribution, and legal usage are governed by the **Publishing, Copyright & Licensing Manual**.

Where another Engineering Handbook governance manual defines policies affecting pricing, both manuals should be interpreted together within their respective scopes.

---

### 8. Future Governance

As the Engineering Handbook ecosystem expands, additional pricing policies may be introduced through officially approved revisions.

New policies should remain consistent with the pricing principles established by this manual.

---

### 9. Decision Summary

Pricing governance ensures that every commercial pricing decision is:

- documented;
- consistent;
- reviewable;
- transparent; and
- aligned with the long-term objectives of the Engineering Handbook ecosystem.
