# Computer Networks Engineering Handbook

## Volume I — Foundation

**Learning Level:** Foundation

**Theme**

Why Networks Exist → Routing and the Global Internet

**Objective**

Build a strong conceptual foundation in computer networking by understanding why networks exist, how information travels across physical media, how Ethernet enables local communication, how Internet Protocol provides global addressing, and how routing interconnects billions of devices into a single worldwide network. Upon completing this volume, readers will possess the mental models required to understand packet delivery from the physical medium to Internet-scale routing.

### Volume I Summary

Volume I establishes the conceptual foundation of computer networking by progressing from the reasons networks exist to the engineering principles that enable global Internet communication. Readers begin with networking fundamentals and protocol architecture before exploring physical communication, Ethernet and local area networking, Internet Protocol addressing, subnetting, IPv4 and IPv6, and finally the routing technologies that interconnect autonomous systems into the modern Internet.
